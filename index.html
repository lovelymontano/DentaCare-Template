FAQ

Common
Questions

Everything you need to know before your first visit.

How often should I visit the dentist?
Most adults benefit from a check-up and professional cleaning every six months. Patients with gum disease or a history of cavities may need to come in every three to four months. During your first visit, we'll recommend the right schedule for you.
Is teeth whitening safe for sensitive teeth?
Yes. We use sensitivity-formulated bleaching gels and desensitizing agents applied before and after the procedure. Most patients with sensitivity experience little to no discomfort during or after treatment.
Do you accept walk-in patients?
Walk-ins are welcome but subject to availability. Booking in advance guarantees your preferred time slot. For dental emergencies, we always reserve same-day slots — just call us and we'll fit you in.
What HMO plans do you accept?
We are accredited with Maxicare, MediCard, Intellicare, PhilCare, Asianlife, and most major HMO providers. We also accept PhilHealth for eligible procedures. Contact us to verify your specific plan coverage.
How long does a dental implant procedure take?
The implant placement itself takes 30–60 minutes per tooth. Osseointegration (bone bonding) takes 3–6 months, after which the crown is attached. The total timeline from start to final crown is typically 4–8 months depending on bone density and healing.
Is the clinic child-friendly?
Absolutely. Our waiting area has a dedicated play corner with toys and tablets. Our pediatric dentists use the Tell-Show-Do technique and child-friendly language to make every visit positive and stress-free for young patients aged 2 and up.
